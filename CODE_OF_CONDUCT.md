# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We want participation in Rate Limit Tomato to be safe, respectful, and welcoming. Contributors, maintainers, and users are expected to treat one another with patience and professionalism regardless of background, identity, experience, or point of view.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful and specific when giving feedback.
- Assume good intent while addressing the actual technical or community impact.
- Welcome questions and help newcomers find relevant documentation.
- Keep discussions focused on the project and its users.
- Respect privacy and do not publish another person's personal information.
- Accept maintainer decisions gracefully, including requests to pause or move a discussion.

## Unacceptable behavior

Harassment, threats, discrimination, sexualized attention, personal attacks, deliberate disruption, impersonation, doxxing, and repeated unwanted contact are not acceptable. Publishing private vulnerability details before coordinated disclosure is also prohibited.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, release channels, and other spaces where someone represents this project.

## Reporting and enforcement

When it is safe to do so, report project conduct concerns to repository maintainers through GitHub. Do not include sensitive personal details in a public issue. For confidential or urgent platform abuse, use GitHub's built-in **Report content** or **Report abuse** workflow.

Maintainers may edit or remove content, close or lock discussions, reject contributions, restrict participation, or escalate platform abuse. Enforcement decisions should consider context, impact, prior behavior, and the safety of affected people. Retaliation against a good-faith reporter is prohibited.

This project does not use the security vulnerability channel for ordinary conduct disputes.
